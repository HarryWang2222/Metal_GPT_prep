Abstract: Polycrystalline Co3Ti (Ll2 ordered structure) was shown deformable in tension between room temperature and 1050°C for compositions between 20 and 23 at.% Ti. The elongation is a maximum around 400°C, and a minimum around 800°C, and increases at higher temperature. There is a correlation between ductility and fracture mode: the transgranular fracture mode results in higher ductility.
