Diffusion induced recrystallization of NiO,
Acta Metallurgica,
Volume 32, Issue 1,
1984,
Pages 29-33,
ISSN 0001-6160,
https://doi.org/10.1016/0001-6160(84)90198-6.
(https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/0001616084901986)
Abstract: It is shown that changing the composition of a sample of NiO from that in equilibrium with air at 1200°C to that in equilibrium with oxygen saturated Ni at 800–900°C will recrystallize the surface to a much finer grain size. Annealing back at 1200°C in air will again recrystallize the surface layer. This type of diffusion induced recrystallization has often been observed in metals, but has never before been reported in ceramics. It's occurrence in NiO is interpreted as a demonstration that diffusion induced grain boundary motion is driven directly by the free energy of mixing defects into the matrix instead of indirectly as suggested by others.
Résumé
Nous montrons que le changement de la composition d'un échantillon de NiO de la composition en l'équilibre dans l'air á 1200°C á celle qui est en équilibre dans Ni saturé en oxygène i 800–900°C produit une recristallisation de la surface à beaucoup plus petite échelle. Si l'on recuit ensuite á 1200°C à l'air, la couche superficielle recristallise de nouveau. Ce type de recristallisation induite par diffusion a été souvent observé dans les métaux, mais il n'avait jamais été rapporté dans les céramiques. Nous interprétons son existence dans NiO comme la démonstration que le mouvement des joints de grains induit par diffusion est produit directement par l'énergie libre des défauts de mélange dans la matrice et non indirectement comme le pensent d'autres auteurs.
Zusammenfassung
Wird die Zusammensetzung einer NiO-Probe geändert, indem sie aus dem Gleichgewicht mit Luft bei 1200°C in ein Gleichgewicht mit sauerstoffgesättigtem Ni bei 800–900°C gebracht wird, dann rekristallisiert die Oberfläche mit viel feinerer Korngröβe. Ausheilen bei 1200°C an Luft bringt die Oberfläche in den alten Zustand zurück. Diese Art der diffusionsinduzierten Rekristallisation wurde sehr oft in Metallen gefunden, wurde jedoch noch nie für Keramiken berichtet. Das Auftreten in NiO wird als ein Hinweis darauf angesehen, daβ die diffusionsinduzierte Korngrenzwanderung direkt durche die freie Energie bewirkt wird, die durch Mischen der Defekte in die Matrix gewonnen wird, statt-wie sonst vorgeschlagen-indirekt.

A new kinetic model for anisothermal metallurgical transformations in steels including effect of austenite grain size,

ISSN 0001-6160,

Abstract: In this paper, we propose a new kinetic model for anisothermal metallurgical transformations, valid for diffusion controlled as well as martensitic transformations. It is first described in the case of two phases and a unique transformation, then extended to the case of n phases and several (possibly simultaneous and unsymmetrical) transformations. The effect of austenite grain size can also be included; an evolution equation for austenite grain size is then needed. We show that the classical equation is limited to the case of constant austenite proportion, and propose a reasonable extension of this classical equation to the general case.
Résumé

Il est d'abord décrit dans le cas de deux phases et d'une transformation unique, puis étendu au cas de n phases et de plusieurs transformations (éventuellement simultanées et non symétriques). On peut aussi tenir compte de l'influence de la taille du grain austénitique; en ce cas, on a besoin d'une équation d'évolution pour cette taille de grain. Nous montrons que l'équation classique est limitée au cas d'une proportion d'austénite constante, et proposons une extension raisonnable de cette équation classique au cas général.
Zusammenfassung

Abstract: The local density of states (LDOS) of electrons at grain boundaries of iron with or without segregation (phosphorus or boron) was calculated. The grain boundary structures constructed by using empirical atomic pair potentials were used as the model systems. The hopping integrals were calculated by using the linear-muffin-tin orbitals and atomic sphere approximation. The atomic level of the individual atomic site near the grain boundary is determined so as to maintain the charge neutrality at each atomic site. Following results were obtained: 1.(1) In the grain boundaries of iron without impurities, the non-bonding orbitals are formed at the Fe-3d bands at the grain boundary, while LDOS's of the neighboring atoms have the bonding orbitals perpendicular to the boundary.2.(2) When boron atoms segregate at the boundary, the non-bonding states of the iron atom disappear by forming the strong bonding orbital with the B-2p orbital, and the grain boundary is strengthened.3.(3) In case of the phosphorus segregation, the non-bonding states are formed at LDOS's of Fe-3d electrons surrounding the Fe9P cluster, and the Fe-Fe bonds are weakened, although the Fe-P bonds in the Fe9P cluster are strengthened.4.(4) The binding character is determined not only directly by the nature of impurity, but also indirectly by the structural change induced by the impurity segregation. Especially in the case of phosphorus segregation, the structural change reduces the bonding character of the host atoms.

A.I. Shirley, C.K. Hall,
Trapping of hydrogen by metallic substitutional impurities in niobium, vanadium, and tantalum,
Acta Metallurgica,
Volume 32, Issue 1,
1984,
Pages 49-56,
ISSN 0001-6160,
https://doi.org/10.1016/0001-6160(84)90201-3.
(https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/0001616084902013)
Abstract: The elastic interaction of hydrogen with substitutional metallic impurities in niobium, vanadium and tantalum has been calculated using lattice statics. The elastic interaction is found to be attractive at the 1st and 2nd nearest neighboring sites to the impurity if the impurity causes the host lattice to expand and is repulsive at these distances if the impurity contracts the lattice. The electronic interaction energy between hydrogen and substitutional impurities is also estimated using a crude phenomenological model. The electronic interaction is found to be attractive if the metal impurity has an electron deficit with respect to the host, and is repulsive if the impurity has an electron excess. Because of the general relationship between atomic size and number of valence electrons, the two interactions tend to occur in a complementary fashion, giving the “rule of thumb” that impurities to the left of the host in the periodic table trap hydrogen and those to the right do not. It is concluded that elastic and electronic interaction energies both play an important role in determining whether substitutional impurities trap, in marked contrast to the case of interstitial impurities where it has been found that trapping is due primarily to elastic interactions. The effect of substitutional trapping on hydrogen solubility in the host is discussed. It is concluded that trapping has only a secondary effect on solubility changes; rather the primary effect appears to be changes in the host's elastic properties upon addition of the substitutional impurity.
Résumé
Nous avons calculé l'interaction élastique de l'hydrogène avec des impuretés métalliques substitutionnelles dans le niobium, le vanadium et le tantale, à l'aide de la statique des réseaux. Nous avons trouvé que l'interaction élastique est attractive pour les premiers et seconds voisins d'une impureté si celle-ci provoque une dilatation du réseau de la matrice et qu'elle est répulsive si l'impureté provoque une contraction de ce réseau. Nous avons également estimé l'énergie d'interaction électronique entre l'hydrogène et les impuretés substitutionnelles, à l'aide d'un modèle phénoménologique grossier. L'interaction électronique est attractive si l'impureté métallique présente un déficit électronique par rapport à l'atome de la matrice; elle est répulsive si l'impureté présente un excès électronique. Du fait de la relation générale entre la taille atomique et le nombre d'électrons de valence, les deux interactions tendent à se produire de manière complémentaire, ce qui conduit à la règle grossière selon laquelle les impuretés situées à gauche de l'atome de la matrice dans la classification périodique piègent l'hydrogène, alors que celles situées à droite ne le piègent pas. Nous concluons que les énergies d'interactions élastique et électronique jouent toutes deux un rôle important dans la détermination de l'effet de piège d'une impureté substitutionnelle, par opposition au cas des impuretés interstitielles pour lesquelles le piègeage est essentiellement dû aux interactions élastiques.
Zusammenfassung
Mit Gitterstatik wurde die elastische Wechselwirkung des Wasserstoffs mit metallischen substitutioneilen Verunreinigungen in Niob, Vanadium und Tantal berechnet. Sie ist anziehend für nächst und übernächst benachbarte Verunreinigungsatome, wenn die Verunreinigung das Wirtsgitter ausdehnt. Sie ist abstoβend bei kontrahierender Wirkung der Verunteinigung. Mit einem groben phänomenologischen Modell wird die elektronische Wechselwirkungs-energie zwischen Wasserstoff und substitutioneller Verunreinigung abgeschätzt. Sie ist anziehend, wenn die metallische Verunreinigung ein Elektronendefizit im Vergleich zum Wirtsgitter aufweist, sie ist dagegen abstoβend bei Elektronenüberschuβ. Wegen des allgemeinen Zusammenhanges Zwischen Atomdurchmesser und Anzahl der Valenzelektronen neigen die beiden Wechselwirkungsarten zugemeinsamem Auftreten. Daraus folgt als “Richtschnur”, daβ die Verunreinigungen im periodischen System links vom Wirtsatom den Wasserstoff eingangen, die rechts dagegen nicht. Also sind elastische und elektronische Wechselwirkungsenergien wichtig für die Wirkung der Substitutionellen Falle. Dieser Befund steht in starkem Kontrast zu interstitiellen Verunreinigungen, deren Fallenwirkung in erster Linie von elastischer Wechselwirkung herrührt.

Abstract: Electron diffraction and microscopy studies of the incommensurate phase in a Ti58.7Ni37.5Al3.8 alloy have been made. Diffuse electron scattering appears as streaks, arcs or interlocking rings in the electron diffraction patterns of the incommensurate phase. The same electron diffraction effects arose from the incommensurate phase which was produced either by increasing the temperature from below to above Td, the onset temperature of the “incommensurate-commensurate” transition, or by the electron beam heating effect. The diffuse electron scattering in the diffraction patterns is interpreted to be due to the intersections of the Ewald sphere and non-spherical, curved surfaces in reciprocal space which may relate to the Fermi surface.
Résumé

Les mêmes effets se produisent dans la phase incommensurable qu'elle soit produite en élevant la température au dessus de Td (température du début de la transition “incommensurable-commensurable”) ou qu'elle soit due à l'effet thermique du faisceau d'électrons. Nous pensons que l'intensité électronique diffuse provient des intersections entre la sphère d'Ewald et des surfaces courbes non sphériques de l'espace réciproque, qui pourraient être liées à la surface de Fermi.

Abstract: A statistical approach has been applied to particle coarsening during liquid phase sintering assuming direct particle coalescence as basic growth mechanism instead of Ostwald ripening. The coalescence process controlled by diffusion through the melt results in an increase of the average particle size proportional to the cube root of sintering time. After a short initial sintering interval the particle size distribution approaches a unique normalized form which is considerably broader than forms predicted by Ostwald ripening theories. The effect of preferred coalescence possibilities for definite particle size ranges and the effect of concurrent coalescence and Ostwald ripening are treated and discussed.

Abstract: An analysis of the probability distribution function of particles randomly dispersed in a solid has been applied to cavitation during superplastic deformation and a method of predicting cavity coalescence developed. Cavity size distribution data were obtained from quantitative metallography on specimens of two microduplex nickel-silver alloys deformed superplastically to various extents, at elevated temperature, and then compared to theoretical predictions from the model. Abstract: A new method is developed for the search of mechanically stable configurations of symmetric tilt boundaries in hard sphere f.c.c. crystals. The problem of finding out relative displacements which minimize the total volume of two crystal blocks forming a boundary, is simplified to a problem of positioning a single atom sphere relative to a block which consists of real and “image” atoms; the lattter are placed in such a way to reflect the arrangement in the other crystal block. The method (the image atom method) has been applied to the analysis of symmetric coincidence boundaries with [100], [110] and [111] tilt axes with ∑-values 3–103. For the [100] (013)∑5 boundary, the procedure of the analysis is described in detail; the derived structures are compared with those by computer simulation. Abstract: Stacking faults associated with climb-dissociated basal and prism plane dislocations in sapphire (α-Al2O3) after plastic deformation at elevated temperatures have been studied. In both cases, the fault vector is 13 〈101&#x0304;0〉 and it is only the cation sublattice which is faulted. The fault energy on and planes are similar, between 0.1 and 0.25 J/m2. Analysis of the geometry of the 13 〈101&#x0304;0〉 fault show that one vacancy and two distinct interstitial faults are possible in this structure; one of the interstitial faults is thought to have the lowest energy.
