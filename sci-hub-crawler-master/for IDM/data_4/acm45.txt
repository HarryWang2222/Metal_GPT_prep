Abstract: Polycrystalline molybdenum 34%(at.) rhenium has been deformed by cold rolling. The variation in twin and dislocation microstructures produced by longitudinal, transverse, cross and pack rolling is reported. Deformation is initially accompanied by much twinning; twin formation being more difficult when the material is rolled in a direction transverse to the original rolling direction. A dislocation cell structure begins to form after about 10 per cent deformation by single direction rolling, but forms at a lower deformation by rolling in two directions. Within limits the dislocation cell size and wall thickness can be varied by the use of different rolling schedules. The structure of the pack rolled material is shown to be due to constraints imposed by the jacket. Failure of this material results from the growth of voids under an internal tension.

Abstract: Deformation behavior and deformation-induced substructures in partially and fully oxidized dilute Ag-Mg and Ag-Cd alloy single crystals, oriented for single slip, have been investigated. The observed stress-strain curves of the partially oxidized crystals can be satisfactorily described by assuming that the load carried by the crystal is sum of the loads supported by the hard exterior and the soft interior. From the substructural examination it is inferred that in the soft region of the partially oxidized crystals stage III occurs at a lower strain level than in the unoxidized crystals. This observation has been rationalized as follows. During the successive stages of loading of the partially oxidized crystals, the hard exterior constrains the soft interior from deforming much beyond the elastic strain of the former. However, when the yield load of the dispersion hardened layer is reached, the composite crystal could yield macroscopically. Since the volume fraction of the second phase particles is moderately high, the hardened layer is likely to deform, right from the start, by multiple slip. For the two regions to undergo compatible deformation, the soft interior should also deform by multiple slip. The required dislocation distribution can evolve, in a very small strain increment, by the net exodus of the glide dislocations from the hard exterior to the soft interior.

Au cours des stades successifs de charge des cristaux partiellement oxydés, l'extérieur dur empêche l'intérieur doux de se déformer beaucoup plus que lui-même. Abstract: Specimens of polycrystalline niobium and molybdenum have been strained in compression at constant strain rates from 1.7 × 10−4 to 102 sec−1 and at temperatures from 77 to 600°K. Both materials exhibited a definite yield point, though in niobium the yield drop became small at the lower temperatures. Results are presented showing the variation of the lower yield stress and the flow stress at 8 per cent strain with strain rate and temperature and for molybdenum data are given concerning the stress at which twinning first occurred. The experimental data are discussed in relation to the theory of thermally activated flow and it is shown that the results cannot be described in terms of a single mechanism unless the apparent activation energy depends on temperature as well as stress. Several possible interpretations of this are examined, of which two are considered the most reasonable: first, an activation entropy that is stress and temperature dependent; secondly, a change from one rate-controlling mechanism to another as the temperature is increased.
