Abstract: Fatigue at long lives was studied in materials of different grain sizes. Alpha brass, copper and aluminum were tested in tension-compression at constant stress-amplitude over a life range of 104–107 cycles. Grain size, d, was varied by at least a factor of ten and beyond 105 cycles had no effect on life in copper and aluminum. In brass, however, an order of magnitude decrease in d increased life by about the same amount. The stress for a given life, σ, was found to be proportional to d−12 in brass. Cracks were examined in longitudinal sections, and the average crack depth, p̄, was proportional to d in brass. That dependence was the basis for concluding that the grain-size effect came from stage I cracking. The differences in behavior between brass, and copper and aluminum, were traced to the differences in slip character of these materials. Cross-slip is easy in copper and aluminum, and a dislocation cell structure is formed in fatigue. In brass, difficult cross-slip limits the structure to transgranular bands of dislocations. The cell structure acts to mask the grain boundaries so that the stage I crack is not affected by grain size. In brass, however, crack growth is impeded near grain boundaries. Increasing grain size in brass lowers the frequency with which the crack encounters boundaries; more rapid stage I crack growth and shortened life follow from this. The end of stage I cracking in all materials appears to occur under a critical value of σ2p̄, which is a measure of the crack driving force.

Abstract: This paper surveys some ideas on the glass forming tendency of monatomic systems. According to the free volume model, glass formation in such systems would result primarily from “jamming” due to the action of repulsive forces. From this viewpoint the metals most prone to glass formation should be those in which the repulsive part of the pair potential is, relatively, the largest; these are the noble, transition and polyvalent metals. Actually the most stable metal glasses formed so far are alloys of noble or transition metals (A) with compositions predominantly in the range A3B to A5B where B is a metalloid. The A elements may form a skeleton having a Bernal dense random packed structure which may be somewhat stabilized by filling its holes with metalloid atoms in the manner suggested by Polk. Some additional ideas on the effect of alloying on metal glass forming tendency are discussed.

Abstract: Decomposition of the eutectoids Co3Si and the β-phase in the Ni-In system, under unidirectional cooling conditions, gives aligned lamellar specimens. Unidirectional solidification of a melt of eutectpid composition in a floating zone or a Bridgman crystal growth apparatus provides the starting material. The decomposition is achieved using the same equipment. The relationship between interlamellar period λ and pulling rate R is measured in a wide range of R's. It is found that λn.R = const, with n = 4. A model of grain boundary diffusion is used to explain this unexpected relationship. The influence of the supercooling on the process is caused by the tendency for the disproportionation process to achieve local equilibrium between starting material and product phases.
Résumé
La décomposition des eutectoïdes Co3Si et de la phase β du système Ni-In, par refroidissement suivant une seule direction, donne des échantillons lamellaires alignés. La solidification, suivant une direction, d'un mélange de composition eutectoïde dans une zone fondue ou dans un appareil de croissance de cristaux de Bridgman fournit le matériau de départ. La décomposition est ensuite achevée avec le même dispositif. Des mesures ont été effectuées pour déterminer la relation existant entre la période interlamellaire λ et la vitesse de croissance R pour un large domaine de R. Les auteurs trouvent que λnR = constante, où n = 4. Un modèle de diffusion aux joints de grains est utilisé pour expliquer cette relation inattendue. L'effet d'une surfusion sur le processus est dû à la tendance du processus de disproportionnation à achever l'équilibre local entre le matériau de départ et les phases obtenues.
Zusammenfassung
Die Entmischung der Eutektoide Co3Si und der β-Phase im Ni-In-System unter gerichteten Abkühlungsbedingungen führt zu ausgerichteten lamellaren Proben. Gerichtetes Erstarren einer Schmelze mit eutektoider Zusammensetzung in einem “floating zone”- oder in einem Bridgman-Kristallzuchtofen liefert das Ausgangsmaterial. Die Entmischungsexperimente wurden in denselben Öfen durchgeführt. Der Zusammenhang zwischen der interlamellaren Periode λ und der Kristallzuchtgeschwindigkeit R wurde in einem groβen R-Bereich gemessen. Es zeigt sich, daβ λnR = constant gilt, wenn n = 4. Dieser nicht erwartete Zusammenhang wird anhand der Korngrenzendiffusion erklärt. Der Einfluβ der Unterkühlung auf den Vorgang ist eine Folge der Tendenz des Disproportionalisierungsprozesses, lokales Gleichgewicht zwischen Ausgangsmaterial und Produktphase zu erreichen.
