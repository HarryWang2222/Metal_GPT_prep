Abstract: Large Widmanstatten particles of θ phase in an Al-5% Cu alloy were grown by annealing for long times at 464°C. Abstract: Growth rates (v) were measured as a function of supercooling (ΔT) on five different crystal faces of high-purity gallium in glass capillary tubes. The parameters of the lines agree reasonably well with those calculated from classical two-dimensional nucleation theory if it is assumed, first, that the two lines intersect at the point at which a new nucleus is formed just as the layer growing laterally from the preceding nucleus is completed, and second, that nucleation occurs only at the periphery of the solid-liquid interface rather than randomly on the crystal face. Das ist mit früheren elektronenmikroskopischen Beobachtungen konsistent, wonach die Teilchen kubische Gestalt haben.
