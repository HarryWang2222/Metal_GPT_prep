Abstract: The rate of growth, interlamellar spacing and proportion of the equilibrium amount of precipitate phase formed were measured as a function of temperature (−13° to 45°C) and misorientation about 〈001〉 (θ = 15° to 25°) in oriented bicrystals of Pb-7 at.% Sn. These data were analyzed on the basis of the Turnbull and the Cahn theories of the cellular reaction and of certain modifications of these theories. The cell boundary diffusivities (DB) calculated at θ = 25° from the modified Turnbull theory fell closest to the radiotracer grain boundary diffusivity data of Pb in Pb-1.55 at.% Sn reported by Stark and Upthegrove. The forms of the dependence of DB upon θ obtained from all theoretical approaches were compatible with the Stark-Upthegrove boundary diffusivity data in pure Pb. The activation energy of the cell boundary mobility, as determined from the Cahn theory, was 5–6 kcal/mole, in remarkably good agreement with published data on grain boundary migration in zone-refined Pb; this result is qualitatively consistent with Cahn's impurity drag theory of boundary migration. Both the Turnbull and the Cahn theories gave thoroughly unsatisfactory results for the interfacial energy of the broad faces of the cell lamellae. The cellular structure failed to develop when θ was reduced from 15° to 13°, in support of C.S. Smith's mechanism of this reaction.

Abstract: The magnetic disaccommodation, i.e., the time dependence of the low field magnetic permeability, was investigated in cobalt base alloys containing 0–5.1 at.% (0–4.2 wt.%) titanium in the temperature range 400–700°C. A strong temperature dependence of the disaccommodation observed in the range 430–530°C could be accounted for predominantly by a single first order relaxation process. Analyzing the data in terms of (a) a single exponential relaxation, (b) a lognormal distribution of relaxation times, and (c) a sum of exponential relaxations, it was shown that the relaxation strength varies as the square of the titanium concentration and that the relaxation is characterized by an activation enthalpy of 69 ± 1 kcal/mole and a τ0 of the order of 10−16 sec. The disaccommodation was attributed to the reorientation of substitutional solute atom (titanium) pairs. From the temperature dependence of the relaxation strength the binding energy of Ti atom pairs was determined to be 0.17 ± 0.08 eV.
Résumé

On observe une forte influence de la température sur le désajustement dans le domaine 430–530°C; on peut en rendre compte principalement par un processus de relaxation simple du premier ordre. Abstract: Periodic Pettier heating and cooling may be generated at a solid/liquid interface by the passage of an alternating electric current. Melting and freezing will then occur at rates which will be influenced by the kinetic law of molecular attachment relating solidification velocity to interface temperature. We describe here the theory of an experiment in which the equation describing the interface attachment kinetics is to be determined by measuring the response of a solid/liquid interface to square-wave Peltier heating and cooling. The mathematical methods required for extracting the kinetic equation by analysis of the waveform of interface motion are given and it is shown that with this technique, linear, parabolic and exponential kinetics should give distinguishably different waveforms. The advantages of the method are discussed, along with the precautions necessary to eliminate possible confusing effects.

Abstract: Similarly oriented single crystals of iron and Fe-N alloys with six different nitrogen concentrations in solid solution were strained in tension between room temperature and 77°K at a tensile strain rate of 8.3 × 10−5/sec. It was found that at room temperature the critical resolved shear stress (CRSS) is a linear function of the concentration of nitrogen plus residual carbon, and the alloy hardening is 460,000 kgf/cm2 per unit atomic fraction of interstitials. The addition of nitrogen to iron increased the athermal component of flow stress and decreased hte thermal component. This decrease in the thermal component of flow stress caused the CRSS of some Fe-N alloys to be lower than that of the purified iron at some temperatures. The increase in the athermal component is compatible with solid solution hardening theories of Mott and Nabarro. The analysis of activation energy and volume for low temperature deformation revealed that the basic mechanism of thermally activated deformation (Peierls-Nabarro mechanism) was not altered by the addition of nitrogen to iron. Observations of slip lines by light microscopy revealed that cross-slip is more difficult and that the slip bands are coarser and farther apart in Fe-N alloy crystals than in iron.

L'addition d'azote au fer augmente la composante athermique de la contrainte et diminue la composante thermique. Cette décroissance de la composante thermique entraîne à certaines températures l'abaissement de la cission critique de quelques alliages Fe-N jusq'à une valeur plus basse que celle correspondant au fer pur. L'accroissement de la composante athermique est compatible avec les théories de Mott et Nabarro sur le durcissement des solutions solides. L'analyse de l'énergie et du volume d'activation pour les déformations à basse température ont montré que le mécanisme de base de la déformation activée thermiquement (mécanisme de Peierls-Nabarro) n'est pas altéré par addition d'azote au fer. Abstract: F.I.M. observations of the completely imaged (102) plane of dilute Pt base alloys (W-, Pd-, Co-, Ni-, AuPt) have been made. Bright spot concentration equal to the solute concentration was found in the WPt system. Vacant site concentrations equal to the solute concentrations were found for the other systems above 2 at.% nominal solute concentration. On the basis of these observations it was concluded that the bright spots and vacant sites represented the lattice sites of solute atoms in the corresponding binary solid solutions. At and below 2 at.% nominal solute concentration, the vacant site concentration exceeded the solute concentration by about 1 at.%. This discrepancy was not resolved in the present investigation. It was found that solute clustered in the AuPt system and ordered in the WPt system. Relative to the random distribution more solute-solute pairs are found in the AuPt system and less such pairs are found in the other systems studied. It was concluded that a quantitative measurement of short range order in dilute Pt base binary alloys by means of such F.I.M. observations is feasible.

Abstract: A convergent, stable, finite-difference solution has been developed for the case of two-dimensional shape changes due to capillarity-induced surface diffusion and applied specifically to the cases of an infinite line of wires and a wire on a plane. The solution for the wire on a plane shows significant deviation from the earlier, order-of-magnitude solution for a wire on a plane obtained by Kuczynski. The solution for an infinite line of wires is obtained here for the first time. Both cases show that a time “law” is only approximately obeyed in a restricted range of neck sizes and if the conventional formula x / a ~ t1n is employed, where x = neck radius, a == wire radius and t = time, it is found that for the range of most experimental data n varies from ~5.2 to ~6.5 with an average value of ~6, rather than the fixed value of 7 predicted by Kuczynski. This, coupled with other indications, leads to the conclusion that surface diffusion has been the dominant mechanism in all wire-sintering experiments to date and some of the data have been re-evaluated on this basis. The solution also provides a quantitative theoretical basis for the actual neck shapes evolved, shows them to be in agreement with the previous predictions of Mullins and in substantial disagreement with the shapes assumed in the previous order-of-magnitude treatments.

Abstract: The motion of glide dislocations in f.c.c. alloy crystals subjected to a unixial stress is analyzed assuming that the steady-state velocity of each Shockley partial is a function of the total force acting on it per unit length. It is shown that the velocity of an a2 〈110〉 glide dislocation is a function of the average Schmid factor of its Shockley partials. It follows that the usual interpretation of Schmid's Law where yield stress is correlated with the total Burgers vector of the glide dislocation is valid only when the magnitudes of the Burgers vectors of the partial dislocations are equal, as happens to be the case in the f.c.c. lattice.
