Abstract: Measurements of grain boundary grooves and facets were made on polycrystalline specimens heated in air at 700–945°C. The results show that surface diffusion is the dominant mass transport mechanism. The surface diffusivity is given by Ds = D0 exp (−Q>8/kT) where D0 ≈ 106 cm2 sec−1 and Qs = 2.40 ± 0.15 eV. The high values of D0 and Qs, for silver covered by an oxygen monolayer imply that similar values reported for other metals are not typical of clean surfaces. The observed widths of low index facets developed during thermal etching agree with the theory for growth by surface diffusion with fast mass transport across the facet.

Les épaisseurs observées de facettes de bas indice, formées pendant l'attaque thermique, sont en accord avec la théorie de croissance par diffusion de surface avec transport de matière rapide au travers de la facette.

Abstract: Formation, reversion, and reformation of GP zones in Al-5.3 at.% Zn were studied through measurement of electrical resistance. Young's modulus, critical resolved shear stress, and small-angle scattering of X-rays. A definite zone radius of approximately 9 Å, a critical size for scattering of conduction electrons, is associated with the resistance maximum or plateau which is observed during aging. This size is independent of aging temperature or whether the zones form on aging or reaging after reversion. After a long aging treatment, about 14 of the Zn atoms present are associated with zones; after a long reaging treatment, only about 14 are associated with zones. The intensity maximum in the small angle X-ray scattering curve early in the aging process is apparently due to a shell depleted in Zn about each zone. It is due to interference between zones late in the process. Early in the process, the zones are more uniform in size than later on; coarsening occurs after an initial depletion of the matrix. There is no incubation time for aging or reaging. The kinetics of formation of zones on aging and reaging depend upon vacancy concentration and supersaturation. These vary with aging temperature, time, and previous treatment. In the absence of a large excess in vacancies (this is the situation during reaging or late in the direct aging process) the relation of the composition of the solid solution about the zone to the spinodal composition is particularly important and the rate of zone formation decreases with an increase in aging temperature. In the initial stages, when there are large numbers of vacancies, the rate increases with an increase in aging temperature.

Abstract: The oxidation of (001) and (100) faces of pure magnesium single crystals at 2.5 mm Hg oxygen pressure was measured at 400°C and 440°C by means of an all-quartz, high vacuum microbalance. Abstract: It is possible in simple metals under simple alternating strain to identify a systematic process of fatigue damage which begins by formation of pores or voids and continues by the linking of these pores into embryo cracks. Details depend on strain-amplitude. At large amplitudes, pores form especially at the irregular sub-boundaries of marked misorientation that are always produced in the grains by large strains and they link into microcracks that are irregular in shape. At smaller amplitudes, which produce little disorientation in the grains, pores form especially on slip planes and so link into fissured slip-planes. At the still smaller amplitudes of the pseudo-safe range, slip movements disperse in a way that hinders both formation and linking of pores. It is possible, further, to observe how these basic processes are modified when complications of practical interest are introduced; for instance, combined stresses and the variations in amplitude leading to so called cumulative damage. The processes are illustrated by metallographic and electron-microscope studies of copper and brass specimens subjected to alternating torsion.
Résumé

Ce phènomène dèpend dans ses dètails de l'amplitude de la dèformation. Aux grandes amplitudes, les pores se forment principalement aux sous-joints irrèguliers, sèparant des sous-grains d'orientations fortement diffèrentes, sous-joints qui sont toujours produits dans les grains par des dèformations importantes; ces pores se rèunissent en microcriques de forme irrègulière. Aux amplitudes encore plus faible dans la zone de pseudo-sècuritè, les mouvements de glissement ont lieu dans toutes les directions d'une manière telle que la dormation et la rèunion des pores en sont empêchèes. Il est possible aussi d'observer comment ces processus de base sont modifiès quand se prèsentent des complications rencontrèes dans la pratique: par exemple, des tensions et des variations d'amplitudes conduisant á des dommages cumulès. Les auteurs prèsentent des ètudes mètallographiques optiques et èlectroniques d'èchantillons de cuivre et de laiton soumis á une tension alternèe.
Zusammenfassung

Abstract: The proposed Theory of Metal Fatigue is based on the proposition that the fatigue problem is better understood, if attention is focused on the propagation of a “dominant crack” presumed responsible for the ultimate catastrophic failure of the specimens.
