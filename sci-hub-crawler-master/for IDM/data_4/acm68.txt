Abstract: In this paper a new method is described for preparing gray tin single crystals. When mercury-rich tin-mercury alloys are transformed in the temperature-range from −8.2 to −35.2°C single crystals of gray tin are obtained. When the growth occurs at a temperature slightly below −8.2°C these single crystals are relatively perfect and contain only a small amount of mercury. Abstract: The kinetics of recrystallization of polycrystalline copper (99,999% purity) cold-rolled to 98% reduction have been determined by means of an X-ray diffraction technique for the temperature range 80°–170°C at atmospheric pressure and at 42 kilobars.
