Comment on “Accurately measured precipitate-matrix misfit in an AlMgSi alloy by electron microscopy”,
Scripta Materialia,
Volume 126,
2017,
Pages 69-70,
ISSN 1359-6462,
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.scriptamat.2016.07.025.
(https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1359646216303360)
Abstract: Drawing attention to a recently reported [S. Wenner, R. Holmestad, Scripta Mater. 118 (2016) 5] close agreement of experimentally and theoretically determined strain fields for the AlMgSi alloy precipitate β″ in fcc Al, we argue that these results may indicate a non-negligible β″/Al misfit along the precipitate main growth direction. The consequences of such findings are discussed.
Keywords: First-principle calculation; Scanning/transition electron microscopy (STEM); Aluminium alloys; Internal stresses; Multiscale modelling

Sisi Xiang, Haibo Long, Shengcheng Mao, Yinong Liu, Hua Wei, Jianxin Zhang, Zhenju Shen, Hongyu Zhang, Xinguang Wang, Ze Zhang, Xiaodong Han,
Response to “Comments on ‘Selective evolution of secondary γ′ precipitation in a Ni-based single crystal superalloy both in the γ matrix and at the dislocation nodes’”,
Scripta Materialia,
Volume 129,
2017,
Pages 104-106,
ISSN 1359-6462,
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.scriptamat.2016.10.013.
(https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1359646216305085)
Abstract: In their comment (Scripta Mater. (2016)), Cormier et al. raised questions on the findings reported in our earlier publication (Acta Mater. (2016) of the formation of 2nd γ′d and 2nd γ′γ precipitates in a Ni-based single crystal superalloy during creep testing at elevated temperatures, and suggested that the 2nd γ′d particles were mere observations of surface protuberances of the primary γ′ rafts and that the 2nd γ′γ precipitates were possibly formed during cooling after the creep test. To clarify these, we conducted new experiments, and present the new evidence and our responses to the points raised in this short communication.
Keywords: Ni-based superalloys; Precipitates

Jonathan Cormier, Vincenzo Caccuri, Jean-Briac le Graverend, Patrick Villechaise,
Comments on ‘Selective evolution of secondary γ′ precipitation in a Ni-based single crystal superalloy both in the γ matrix and at the dislocation nodes’,
Scripta Materialia,
Volume 129,
2017,
Pages 100-103,
ISSN 1359-6462,
https://doi.org/10.1016/j.scriptamat.2016.10.012.
(https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1359646216305073)
Abstract: In a recent paper, Sisi Xiang & al. [Acta Materialia 116 (2016) 343] studied microstructure evolutions of γ′ precipitates during creep of a Ni-based single crystal superalloy, with a special focus on hyperfine γ′ particles nucleating in the γ matrix channels. This paper is misleading, claiming that hyperfine γ′ particles nucleate during high temperature creep exposure at 1100°C. The aim of the present comments is to reconsider these results based on the available literature on hyperfine γ′ particles nucleation mechanisms and subsequent morphological evolutions at high temperature.
Keywords: Ni-based single crystal superalloys; Creep; Hyperfine γ′ precipitation
