Abstract: Here we report the finding that the strongest grain size to achieve the maximum flow stress in nanocrystalline Ni nanowires was significantly smaller (d=5nm) than that in typical bulk metals (d>10nm). The discrepancy was due to the dominance of plasticity via surface slip in Ni nanowires at relatively large grain sizes, which was absent in bulk materials. An interesting grain size- and temperature-dependent “hardening”-to-“softening” transition in Ni nanowires was also revealed in this study.
Keywords: Nanocrystalline; Nanowire; Grain size; Plasticity

Electropulse-induced microstructural evolution in a ferritic–pearlitic 0.14% C steel,
Scripta Materialia,

(https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1359646214004084)
Abstract: The present work reports the experimental observation of electropulse-induced microstructural evolution in a ferritic–pearlitic steel at ambient temperature. Electropulsing initially causes the fragmentation of lamellar structure. Further treatment leads to the formation of new cementite plates aligned with the current direction. This is attributed to the reduction of the system free energy. The hardness of the material decreased with an increase in the number of electric current pulses. Electrical resistivity is thought to be responsible for the observed phenomenon.
Keywords: Ferritic–pearlitic steels; Electropulsing; Microstructural evolution; Electrical properties
