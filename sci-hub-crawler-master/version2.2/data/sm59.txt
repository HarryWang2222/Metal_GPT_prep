Abstract: The behaviour of stainless steel hollow sphere foams under uniaxial tensile loading is characterized both at the macroscopic scale and at the scale of the individual cell. Abstract: Tensile properties of a supersaturated carbon layer (∼7.5at.%) in AISI 304 grade stainless steel have been evaluated using in situ synchrotron diffraction. A small incident beam enabled diffraction from within the enriched layer and the unaffected material during tensile deformation. The elevated carbon level was also found to have significantly softened the diffraction elastic constants of the enriched layer when compared to the parent material.
